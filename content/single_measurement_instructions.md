To calculate the economic loss due to poor quality for a single measurement enter the Upper Specification Limit (USL), Lower Specification Limit (LSL), cost of scrap, and the measured value for the performance or quality characteristic in the fields below.

As a default, the USL is 33 units, the LSL is 27 units, the cost of scrap is \$5.00, and the measured quality characteristic is 28 units. Given these parameters, the tolerance is 6 units, the target is 30 units, the value for K is \$0.56, and the expected loss is $2.22.

Note that, although this model uses symmetrical specification limits, the Taguchi loss function can also be applied when working with one-sided (asymmetrical) limits.
